Starliner Attempts Second Launch To The ISS

After numerous delays, Boeing's CST-100 Starliner capsule lifted off atop an Atlas V N22 on May 20th 8:54am AEST, headed for the International Space Station (ISS). The docking is set for 21st of May, 9:10am AEST. The launch was a huge success, with the Atlas V core stage even overperforming! The capsule will stay at the station for several days, allowing the teams to gather data from the capsule, and the astronauts to take the food and supplies out of the capsule, before the capsule undocks, and lands under parachute and airbags.

What Happened To The Last Launch?

The last launch was a success, however, the Atlas did not put the capsule in the designated orbit, ending in the capsule missing the ISS, and having to re-enter the atmosphere shortly after launch.
